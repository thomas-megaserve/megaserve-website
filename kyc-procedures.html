Know Your Client (KYC) Procedures

Documented, risk-based procedures your teams can execute: CDD/EDD/SDD flows, identification & verification, UBO mapping, triggers & refresh cycles, and GDPR-aligned recordkeeping.

What We Do

  • Define KYC policy scope: customer types, jurisdictions, products, and risk appetite.
  • Codify CDD/EDD/SDD procedures with clear evidence thresholds and templates.
  • Identification & verification steps (certification/legalisation rules included).
  • Ownership & control mapping: multi-layer UBO tracing and documentation.
  • Screening linkages to sanctions/PEP/adverse media and approval routing.
  • Trigger events & periodic refresh schedules by risk tier (e.g., 12/24/36 months).
  • Record retention, privacy notices, and access controls aligned to GDPR/policy.
  • Governance: exception handling, change control, and versioned approvals.

KYC Levels & Evidence at a Glance

Level / Scenario Identification Ownership & Control Screening Approvals & Refresh
Simplified Due Diligence (SDD) Basic ID & address; certification if policy requires N/A or minimal confirmation Sanctions/PEP baseline Line manager sign-off; 36m refresh
Customer Due Diligence (CDD) Verified IDs for client & signatories; PoA ≤3–6m Shareholder/BO registers; org chart; ≥25% UBOs Sanctions/PEP + adverse media Compliance approval; 24m refresh
Enhanced Due Diligence (EDD) Certified IDs; notarisation/legalisation if required Full multi-layer UBO tracing; SoW/SoF with evidence Expanded adverse media sources; heightened checks MLRO sign-off; 12m refresh
Trigger Event (change/negative news) Re-confirm affected identities Update ownership where relevant Targeted re-screening Out-of-cycle review & memo

Exact levels/evidence depend on your policy and counterpart requirements. We implement your standards and document exceptions.

Process

  1. Scope & Baseline: Confirm customer types, jurisdictions, risk appetite, and current practices.
  2. Design Procedures: Draft CDD/EDD/SDD flows, evidence thresholds, and approval routing with templates.
  3. Integrate Screening: Map sanctions/PEP/adverse-media steps and escalation notes into the flow.
  4. Pilot & Train: Run sample files, refine thresholds, publish playbooks, and brief teams.
  5. Approve & Publish: Versioned sign-offs, change log, and distribution to users.
  6. Review & Refresh: Set periodic reviews and register of exceptions/assumptions for audits.

FAQ

This page is informational and not legal or regulatory advice. Final positions rest with your Compliance/MLRO and advisors.

Are the procedures templates or tailored?
Tailored. We start from proven structures and align to your policy, jurisdictions, products, and counterpart expectations.
Do you provide legal/regulatory advice?
No. We document and operate procedures aligned to your advisors’ guidance and applicable rules. Regulatory interpretations remain with licensed professionals.
How often should KYC be refreshed?
Typically by risk tier (e.g., 12/24/36 months) and on trigger events. Your policy sets exact intervals.
Can procedures integrate with screening and onboarding?
Yes. We embed sanctions/PEP/adverse-media steps and link to onboarding files, approvals, and bank pack requirements.
Who approves exceptions?
Per your approval matrix (e.g., Compliance or MLRO for EDD). We document rationale in the exceptions register.
