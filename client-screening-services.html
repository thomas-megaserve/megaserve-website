Client Screening Services

Stand up or optimise sanctions, PEP, and adverse-media screening with clear playbooks—reducing false positives while preserving control, documentation, and auditability.

What We Do

  • Policy mapping to lists/jurisdictions (UN, EU, UK, OFAC, local, sectoral).
  • Name-matching calibration: thresholds, tokenisation, transliteration, fuzzy rules.
  • PEP classification and close-associate logic aligned to your risk tiers.
  • Adverse media queries, sources, and triage criteria with evidence capture.
  • Alert handling workflows: disposition notes, approvals, escalations, and QA.
  • Periodic refresh schedules by risk (12/24/36m) and event-driven triggers.
  • Vendor/tool administration: list updates, user roles, and change logs.
  • MI & reporting: volumes, hit rates, ageing, and exceptions/registers.

We align configuration and evidence standards to your policy and the receiving bank/authority expectations.

Process

  1. Policy & Scope: Confirm lists, jurisdictions, risk tiers, and approval thresholds; define records and retention.
  2. Calibrate: Configure vendor/tool settings (match scores, aliases, transliteration), user roles, and change control.
  3. Pilot & QA: Run sample batches, tune thresholds, document exceptions, and approve the configuration.
  4. Operate: Triage alerts, capture evidence, escalate potential true positives, and maintain registers.
  5. Report: Weekly/Monthly MI on volumes, hit rates, ageing, and QA findings with actions.
  6. Refresh & Audit: Schedule periodic reviews, perform portfolio sweeps, and compile exam/audit packs.

FAQ

This page is informational and not legal or regulatory advice. Final decisions rest with your Compliance/MLRO and relevant authorities/banks.

Which lists do you screen against?
We align to your policy (e.g., UN, EU, UK, OFAC) and any local/sectoral regimes you specify. Coverage and refresh schedules are documented in the mapping note.
Do you replace our Compliance/MLRO?
No. We operate the workflow and documentation. Regulatory positions and final approvals remain with your designated officers.
Can you guarantee zero false negatives?
No. Screening is risk-based. We tune thresholds, enforce QA, and document assumptions to reduce error while avoiding excessive false positives.
How do you handle adverse-media alerts?
We apply defined sources/keywords, triage hits to cases with evidence links, and escalate material items per policy.
What about data privacy?
We work within your retention policy, minimise data shared, restrict access via roles, and keep an audit trail of who accessed what and when.
