Naturalisation — Years of Residence

We verify eligibility, audit your residence history, assemble the application, liaise with the authorities, and coordinate oath scheduling through to citizenship issuance.

Overview

Naturalisation by years of residence suits long-term residents who meet the statutory stay and good character conditions. MegaServe de-risks the file by reconciling your entry/exit history, residence permits, and tax/insurance footprints before submission.

  • Eligibility memo with residence-days reconciliation
  • Document pack: police clearances, language/knowledge proof (where applicable), civil status
  • Application drafting, filing, and query handling
  • Oath scheduling, registry updates, and ID/passport follow-through
  • Secure evidence repository with audit trail

Factor Baseline Expectation Evidence Examples Notes

Matrix is indicative; exact thresholds and proofs are confirmed during scoping against current practice.

Process

  1. Discovery & scoping — assess route, years of stay, and absences.
  2. Residence audit — reconcile entry/exit logs, permits, tax/insurance records.
  3. Evidence build — police clearances, civil status, translations/legalisations.
  4. Application drafting & filing — forms, declarations, fees, and submission.
  5. Authority liaison — respond to queries; schedule interviews/tests if any.
  6. Decision & oath — coordinate oath ceremony, registry updates, ID/passport.

FAQ

How are qualifying years calculated?
We reconcile your lawful stay and absences against permits and border data, applying the current thresholds for the chosen route.
What happens if I have long absences?
We assess against allowable limits and, if necessary, propose mitigation such as waiting periods or alternative evidence.
Do I need police clearances from multiple countries?
Typically from your home country and Cyprus for the relevant period. Foreign-issued documents often require legalisation and translation.
Is a language or civic test required?
Where applicable, we provide practice resources and schedule the exam, including re-test planning if needed.
How long does the process take?
Preparation is usually 4–12 weeks depending on audits and clearances; authority decision times vary and are outside our control.
