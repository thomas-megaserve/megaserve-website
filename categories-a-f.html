Permanent Residence — Categories A–F

Traditional permanent residence pathways for investors, professionals, and self-sufficient applicants. We scope eligibility, build the filing pack, and manage each stage with full compliance documentation.

Overview

Categories A–F provide alternative residence routes for third-country nationals engaging in business, agriculture, employment, or self-sufficient living in Cyprus. Each category reflects a different economic activity or income base. MegaServe manages the route mapping, evidence preparation, authority filings, and post-approval compliance tracking.

  • Eligibility assessment across all Categories A–F
  • Investment, employment, or self-sufficiency verification
  • Checklist and document preparation with translation/legalisation guidance
  • Application filing and end-to-end authority liaison
  • Renewal reminders and compliance updates

Category Breakdown (Guide)

Category Applicant Type Key Requirements Notes

B Self-employed in mining Valid mining licence; sufficient capital/experience Specialist approvals required

D Self-employed in profession/science Qualifications, premises, sustainable income Local licensing may apply

F Self-sufficient (no work in Cyprus) Stable income/funds abroad; accommodation Popular with retirees and remote earners

Matrix is indicative; exact criteria are confirmed at scoping against current practice.

Process

  1. Initial scoping — determine the most suitable category (A–F) and dependants.

  4. Submission & liaison — file, track, and handle authority queries.

FAQ

How do Categories A–F differ from Reg. 6(2)?
Reg. 6(2) is an accelerated PR route based on qualifying investment. Categories A–F are traditional routes tied to economic activity, employment, or self-sufficiency.
Which category is most common today?
Category F is frequently used for self-sufficient applicants or retirees with income from abroad. We confirm fit during scoping.
Can dependants be included?
Spouses and minor children are generally eligible, subject to income and accommodation criteria. We coordinate linked applications.
What documents usually need legalisation?
Foreign civil status documents (marriage/birth), police clearances, and some financial proofs often require apostille/legalisation and certified translation.
How long does the process take?
Preparation is typically 3–9 weeks depending on category and document readiness. Decision timelines are set by authorities.
