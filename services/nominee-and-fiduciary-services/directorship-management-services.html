Who It’s For

  • International groups that require Cyprus resident directors and documented control to support substance and governance credibility
  • Cyprus companies needing structured board cadence, approvals, and decision records that banks and auditors recognise
  • Owners who want independent governance within board-approved mandates (not “rubber-stamp” arrangements)

What You Get (Deliverables)

  • Appointment documents and updated corporate registers
  • Board calendar and annual approvals schedule
  • Delegated authority & signatory matrix (banking/user roles/limits)
  • Board packs, minutes, resolutions, and decision register
  • Compliance & filings calendar (annual return, UBO/BoR, licenses)
  • Quarterly status note: open actions, risks, upcoming approvals

Process & Typical Timeline

Onboarding Process

  1. Intake: corporate history, business model, counterparties, existing mandates.
  2. Diligence: KYC/KYB for proposed appointees; conflicts check; insurance alignment if applicable.
  3. Mandates: prepare/update board resolutions, PoAs, and bank signatory matrices.
  4. Calendar: set annual board schedule, recurring approvals, and filings reminders.
  5. Go-Live: first meeting held; minutes issued; action log opened and tracked.

Governance Setup

  • Board composition review and role definitions (chair, director, officer, company secretary)
  • Decision inventory and delegated authority matrix (financial, banking, contracts, HR)
  • Meeting logistics: Cyprus venue/virtual, agenda sequence, and supporting papers
  • Policy set: conflicts of interest, related-party transactions, approval thresholds
  • Registers & books: directors, shareholders, charges, and minute books (digital + physical)

Roles & Responsibilities

Roles, scope of work, and boundaries for directorship & management services
Role What We Do Boundaries
Director (Resident) Attend/lead board meetings; review/approve matters within agreed delegations; maintain decision records; ensure filings are initiated. Not a “rubber stamp”; no decisions outside authority; no shadow direction from non-board parties.
Corporate Officer Execute filings, maintain registers/minute books, coordinate with registrar, banks, auditors, and authorities. Does not replace regulated advisors (tax/legal/audit); escalates non-routine matters to the board.
Bank Signatory Implement role/limit matrix; operate dual control; maintain user access list and updates. No personal custody of client funds; actions only per board-approved mandates.

We work within clear delegations approved by your board. Independence and documented decision-making are central to our appointments.

Case Study

International Consortium Governance — Independent Directorial Representation (Anonymised)

An international consortium operated through a Cyprus entity and required genuine, independent local directorial oversight—not a rubber-stamp arrangement. MegaServe provided resident directorial representation, implemented a board cadence, approval thresholds, and decision logging, and ensured actions were taken only within board-approved mandates.

As requests and operational decisions arose across group companies, MegaServe escalated items through the agreed governance route, documented rationale and approvals, and maintained a clear audit trail of decisions and communications.

Result: the Cyprus entity maintained bankable governance and documented control, supporting counterpart confidence and audit readiness.

Requirements (Client Checklist)

  • Certificate set, Memorandum & Articles, current registers, minute book snapshot
  • Shareholder and group structure (current & any intended changes)
  • Existing bank mandates and user lists
  • Delegation of authority / approval thresholds (if any)
  • Key contracts and counterparties summary
  • Policy set (conflicts, related-party, signing policy) if available

Risk & Mitigations

  • Independence: resident directors are not “rubber stamps”; decisions are taken within board-approved mandates with recorded rationale.
  • Delegations: authority matrix and approval thresholds are defined up front to reduce ambiguity and rework.
  • Conflicts: conflicts are declared and documented; recusal is recorded where required with a clear audit trail.
  • Bank/auditor scrutiny: we maintain decision records, registers, and meeting cadence to support bankable operations and audit readiness.

FAQs

Will directors be genuinely involved or only sign documents?

They participate in scheduled meetings, review packs, ask questions, and approve within agreed delegations. We document the decision process.

How are conflicts of interest handled?

Conflicts are declared in advance; conflicted directors recuse from decisions as needed. We maintain a conflicts register.

Do you provide bank signatories?

Yes, within board-approved mandates. We operate dual control and maintain an up-to-date access list.

Can you work with our existing advisors?

Yes. We coordinate with your tax, legal, audit, and finance teams; we do not replace them.

Share any unusual constraints early (counterparties, jurisdictions, approval thresholds). We’ll reflect them in the authority matrix and meeting plan.
