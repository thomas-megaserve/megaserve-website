Who It’s For

  • International owners who need a Cyprus communications presence without full-time premises
  • Cyprus entities that want predictable mail/courier handling and meeting room access during visits
  • Teams that need structured correspondence routing during bank/EMI or audit workflows

What You Get (Deliverables)

  • Business correspondence address for non-statutory use (invoices, websites, counterparties)
  • Dedicated local phone number with call handling and message forwarding
  • Mail intake, scanning, and forwarding; tracked couriers with delivery proofs
  • Meeting room scheduling in Nicosia (hourly / half-day / day)
  • Guest reception, visitor logs, and basic hospitality (water/coffee)
  • On-demand admin support: document packs, printing, certified copy coordination
  • Optional integration with Registered Address and Physical Office

Process & Typical Timeline

Onboarding Flow

  1. Intake: contact matrix, call script (if any), forwarding preferences, and access emails.
  2. Set-Up: local number provisioning, mailbox creation, shared folder, and escalation rules.
  3. Kick-Off: test calls, test scans, and a sample courier dispatch to confirm preferences.
  4. Go-Live: monthly cadence agreed; meeting room access details supplied.

Case Study

Bank/EMI KYB Support via Virtual Office Communications & Audit Trail

Client: International services company with a Cyprus entity (anonymised)

The client was undergoing bank/EMI KYB onboarding and needed a credible Cyprus-facing communications setup. The institution required timely responses to clarifications and a clean record of correspondence.

MegaServe implemented a Cyprus virtual office communications layer (business contact presence + structured escalation rules), logged inbound bank/authority correspondence, scanned and indexed documents, and routed clarifications to the client promptly, maintaining a consistent audit trail (timestamped items, searchable PDFs, delivery proofs where relevant).

Result: clarification requests were handled without missed deadlines, and the client maintained a clean, bankable correspondence record during onboarding. Messages were forwarded within 1 hour during business hours, and mail scans were delivered same day / next business day depending on intake time.

Requirements (Onboarding)

  • Company details (name, registration no., contact matrix)
  • Forwarding addresses/emails and escalation rules
  • Call script (if applicable) and routing instructions
  • Any formatting or confidentiality requirements for scans
  • Meeting room preferences (capacity, equipment)

Risk & Practical Limits

  • Statutory vs. business address: virtual office is for business correspondence. For statutory purposes, use our Registered Address service.
  • Substance: virtual office supports communications and logistics; formal substance (board meetings, premises) is provided under Directorship or Physical Office.
  • Call content: we do not provide legal/tax advice on calls; we route messages per your script and escalation rules.
  • Data handling: mail scans are stored in a structured folder with access controls; retention periods can be configured.

FAQs

Can the virtual office be used as the company’s registered office?

No. Registered office is a separate statutory service. See Registered Address.

Do you provide a local phone number and answer calls in Greek/English?

Yes. We provide a local number and handle calls in EN/GR (RU on request), forwarding messages per your script.

How quickly do you forward urgent mail?

We flag urgent items on intake and can dispatch same-day if confirmed before 14:00.

Can we bundle with board meeting facilities?

Yes. Combine with Directorship for substance (board meetings in Cyprus) and/or Physical Office for dedicated space.

Unusual requirements (after-hours call cover, large consignments, confidentiality protocols)? Include them when you contact us—we’ll tailor the setup.
