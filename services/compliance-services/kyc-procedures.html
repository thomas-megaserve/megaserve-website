Who It’s For

  • Corporate service providers, fiduciary firms, and regulated teams that need consistent CDD/EDD/SDD procedures
  • Businesses onboarding cross-border clients where evidence standards must be bank- and auditor-ready
  • Operators who want clear triggers, refresh cycles, and a defensible approvals framework

What You Get (Deliverables)

  • KYC policy outline and risk-tier framework (CDD/EDD/SDD)
  • Procedures manual with thresholds, routing, and evidence standards
  • Template pack: checklists, approval memos, SoW/SoF forms, UBO mapping guide
  • Exceptions & assumptions register and versioned change log
  • Refresh calendar and roles/RACI summary

Process & Typical Timeline

  1. Scope: confirm customer types, jurisdictions, products, risk appetite, and current practice.
  2. Design: draft CDD/EDD/SDD flows, evidence thresholds, and approval routing with templates.
  3. Integrate: embed sanctions/PEP/adverse-media steps and escalation notes.
  4. Pilot: run sample files, refine thresholds, and finalise the playbooks.
  5. Publish: versioned sign-offs, change log, and distribution to users.
  6. Review: set periodic reviews and registers for audits/exams.

KYC Levels & Evidence (Guide)

Level / Scenario Identification Ownership & Control Screening Approvals & Refresh
Simplified Due Diligence (SDD) Basic ID & address; certification if policy requires Minimal confirmation Sanctions/PEP baseline Line manager sign-off; 36m refresh
Customer Due Diligence (CDD) Verified IDs for client & signatories; PoA ≤3–6m Shareholder/BO registers; org chart; ≥25% UBOs Sanctions/PEP + adverse media Compliance approval; 24m refresh
Enhanced Due Diligence (EDD) Certified IDs; notarisation/legalisation if required Full multi-layer UBO tracing; SoW/SoF with evidence Expanded adverse media sources MLRO sign-off; 12m refresh
Trigger Event (change/negative news) Re-confirm affected identities Update ownership where relevant Targeted re-screening Out-of-cycle review & memo

Indicative only — exact levels and evidence thresholds are set by your policy and counterpart requirements.

Case Study

Independent KYC Review on a Lawyer-Introduced Client (Anonymised)

Client: Corporate client introduced by a legal professional (anonymised)

The client was introduced via a professional intermediary, but MegaServe treated the file as a standard risk-based KYC case — no reliance on the introducer as a substitute for evidence. The objective was to confirm beneficial ownership, source of funds/wealth, and screening outcomes using documented thresholds and approvals.

During the evidence build, MegaServe applied the full CDD flow: identity verification for key persons, UBO tracing across layers, screening checks, and a structured SoW/SoF narrative with supporting documentation. Gaps and inconsistencies were escalated through the approval routing, and the exceptions/assumptions register was used to capture what could not be resolved.

Result: risks were identified early and documented in an audit-ready decision memo, preventing reliance on incomplete third-party assurances and protecting the firm from avoidable exposure.

FAQs

Are the procedures templates or tailored?

Tailored. We start from proven structures and align to your policy, jurisdictions, products, and counterpart expectations.

Do you provide legal/regulatory advice?

No. We document and operate procedures aligned to your advisors’ guidance and applicable rules. Regulatory interpretations remain with licensed professionals.

How often should KYC be refreshed?

Typically by risk tier (e.g., 12/24/36 months) and on trigger events. Your policy sets exact intervals.

Can procedures integrate with screening and onboarding?

Yes. We embed sanctions/PEP/adverse-media steps and link to onboarding files, approvals, and bank pack requirements.

Who approves exceptions?

Per your approval matrix (e.g., Compliance or MLRO for EDD). We document rationale in the exceptions register.
