Who It’s For

  • Firms that need consistent sanctions/PEP/adverse-media screening with documented decisions
  • Teams overwhelmed by false positives who still need defensible controls
  • Operators preparing for bank reviews, audits, or regulator requests

What You Get (Deliverables)

  • Screening scope note (lists/jurisdictions, refresh frequency, approval limits)
  • Name-matching tuning sheet (thresholds, aliases, transliteration rules)
  • Templates for alert triage, escalation, and approval notes
  • Registers: exceptions, decisions, configuration changes
  • Audit pack structure and sample evidence files
  • Monthly reporting on volumes, hit rates, and ageing

Process & Typical Timeline

  1. Scope: confirm lists, jurisdictions, risk tiers, and approval thresholds.
  2. Calibrate: tune matching rules (scores, aliases, transliteration), roles, and change control.
  3. Pilot: run sample cases, capture false positives, adjust and document the final settings.
  4. Operate: triage alerts, capture evidence, escalate credible items, and maintain registers.
  5. Refresh: periodic re-screen by risk tier and event-driven triggers.
  6. Audit: compile packs with evidence, decisions, timestamps, and approvers.

Case Study

PEP Match on Prospective Partner UBO (Anonymised)

Client: Services company screening a prospective partner where the ultimate owner triggered a PEP match (anonymised)

The prospective relationship raised a PEP alert against the partner’s ultimate owner. The key risk was mishandling a possible false match (or ignoring a credible one), leaving the business exposed and the file indefensible to banks or auditors.

MegaServe confirmed identifiers (name variants/aliases, date of birth, nationality), captured screening evidence and sources, and documented a clear triage note with the reasoning and next steps. The case was escalated where required, approvals were recorded, and the decision was logged in the register with timestamps and an evidence pack.

Result: a defensible decision (pause/proceed/escalate) supported by a clean paper trail that a reviewer can audit quickly.

FAQs

Which lists do you screen against?

We align to your policy (for example UN, EU, UK, OFAC) and any local or sectoral regimes you specify. Coverage and refresh schedules are documented in the scope note.

Do you replace our Compliance/MLRO?

No. We operate the workflow and documentation. Regulatory positions and final approvals remain with your designated officers.

Can you guarantee zero false negatives?

No. Screening is risk-based. We tune thresholds, enforce QA, and document assumptions to reduce error while avoiding excessive false positives.

How do you handle adverse-media alerts?

We apply defined sources and criteria, triage hits to cases with evidence references, and escalate material items per policy.

What about data privacy?

We follow your retention policy, minimise data shared, restrict access by role, and keep an audit trail of who accessed what and when.
