Who It’s For

  • Buyers who need disciplined diligence coordination, a clean issues list, and controlled closing mechanics
  • Sellers who want structured information flow (data room, Q&A, version control) and fewer surprises late in the deal
  • Cross-border deals involving Cyprus entities, Cyprus assets, or Cyprus-based operations that need local coordination

What You Get (Deliverables)

  • Mandate brief and critical path workplan
  • Information pack support (teaser/summary materials) and version control
  • Data room structure, indexing, permissions, and Q&A tracking
  • Issues list and decision log for term sheet and SPA/APA negotiations (with your counsel)
  • Signing/closing checklists, settlement steps, and a closing binder
  • Post-close day-1 handover notes and first-90-days action list (where required)

Process & Typical Timeline

  1. Scope & readiness: define objectives, deal perimeter, roles, and document discipline.
  2. Information & valuation note: collate management inputs and prepare an indicative valuation note (where requested).
  3. Diligence coordination: stand up data room and Q&A tracker; coordinate workstreams with advisors.
  4. Docs & approvals: maintain issues list and approvals gates alongside counsel’s drafting.
  5. Closing & handover: run signing/closing checklists; compile closing binder; day-1 handover.

Case Study

Asset Purchase with Lease Assignment and Day-1 Continuity (Anonymised)

Client: International buyer acquiring operational assets and taking over premises via lease/assignment (anonymised)

The client was purchasing operational assets from a Cyprus-based business and needed the transaction to include the premises. The critical path was not only the asset list — it was getting the lease position clean, with the right landlord paperwork and a clear handover so operations could continue without disruption.

MegaServe coordinated the documentation pack and checklists around the asset schedule and the property side: lease documents, landlord requirements, consents/approvals, and the supporting evidence that needed to match the buyer’s onboarding and governance records.

We worked alongside legal counsel and the client’s advisors to keep the process practical: tracking open points, making sure the right documents were signed in the right order, and ensuring the handover file was complete for the buyer’s records and for any bank or auditor follow-up.

Result: the asset transfer and lease handover were executed cleanly, with continuity at the premises and a complete closing file for future compliance and banking queries.

Risk & Mitigations

  • Confidentiality leakage: we use structured access control and a single source of truth for documents and Q&A.
  • Version chaos: strict naming and approval gates prevent “draft drift” and contradictory attachments.
  • Late closing delays: conditions precedent and sign-off dependencies are tracked early, not in the last week.
  • Third-party responsiveness: we keep an owner-based tracker and escalate when blockers appear.

FAQs

Information here is general and not legal, tax, or investment advice. We coordinate with licensed professionals where required.

Do you replace legal counsel or investment banks?

No. We coordinate execution and documentation. Counsel drafts legal instruments (term sheet, SPA/APA). Any regulated investment services are handled by licensed firms.

What deal sizes do you support?

Most commonly SME and mid-market transactions. The correct scope depends on complexity, number of stakeholders, and timeline.

How do you protect confidentiality and data?

Mutual NDAs, a segregated data room with role-based permissions, access logs, and controlled Q&A. Sensitive documents are not shared by email.

How are fees structured?

Fixed-fee modules or monthly retainer for coordination, with optional success components via separate agreement. Disbursements (filings, notarisation, translations) are billed at cost.
