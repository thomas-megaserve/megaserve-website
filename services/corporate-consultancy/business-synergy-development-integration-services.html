Who It’s For

  • Owners and boards who want a controlled integration plan, not vague “synergy” talk
  • Cross-border projects where multiple parties must align on roles, approvals, and reporting
  • Teams that need cutover discipline and measurable benefits tracking, with clear accountability

What You Get (Deliverables)

  • Integration workplan and critical path with owners and target dates
  • Workstream structure and a single reporting cadence (minutes, actions, issues)
  • Cutover plan (runbook), go/no-go gates, and contingency steps
  • Benefits tracking plan: baseline, measures, and reporting rhythm
  • Structured document archive with version control and decisions logged

Process & Typical Timeline

  1. Diagnose: establish baseline performance, current processes/systems, and where benefits could realistically come from.
  2. Design: agree the target operating approach, responsibilities, approval gates, and reporting.
  3. Plan: publish the integration plan, dependency map, and a cutover strategy.
  4. Build & rehearse: prepare materials, test critical steps, and confirm go/no-go criteria.
  5. Cutover & stabilise: execute the runbook, manage issues to closure, and track benefits against baseline.

Case Study

Investor–Shipbuilding Coordination for German Ship Owner (Anonymised)

Client: Investment party working alongside a shipbuilding company on a vessel project for a German ship owner (anonymised)

The project required close coordination between an investor and an operating shipbuilding company, each with different priorities, reporting expectations, and decision timelines. The risk was not technical delivery, but loss of control through fragmented communication and unclear responsibility.

MegaServe structured the cooperation framework, clarified roles between the parties, and implemented a single coordination and reporting rhythm. We acted as the central point of administrative and operational control, ensuring documentation, approvals, and project milestones progressed in a consistent and traceable manner.

Result: the parties operated within a clear governance structure, communication friction was reduced, and the project advanced without administrative delays or control disputes.

FAQs

Do you replace consultants or technical integrators?

No. We run integration coordination and governance. Specialist design/build work remains with your appointed consultants, vendors, and advisors.

How long does integration usually take?

Small integrations can stabilise in weeks; complex integrations can take months. The timeline depends on data quality, system complexity, and how many parties must approve changes.

What makes integrations fail most often?

Poor ownership (no one accountable), unclear approvals, and unmanaged dependencies. We fix those first: roles, gates, and a real plan.

Do you manage communications?

We structure the communications plan and can support internal briefings. External communications are issued by authorised owners.
