Permanent Residence — Categories A–F

We help you choose the correct Category (A–F), prepare the documents, submit the application, and follow up until a decision.

Who It’s For

  • Non-EU nationals applying under Categories A–F
  • Applicants who need clear guidance on the correct category and documents
  • Families who want linked applications and organised records

We also support Category E (employment) where the employment-based route is the correct fit.

What You Get

  • Category confirmation (A–F) and document plan
  • Clear checklist and templates
  • Prepared filing pack and submission receipts
  • Appointment booking help (depends on authority calendars)
  • Status updates and organised records for renewal

Process & Typical Timeline

  1. Check category fit: confirm A–F route, family members, and timing.
  2. Collect documents: we confirm what needs translation/legalisation.
  3. Prepare & submit: forms and supporting evidence.
  4. Follow up: respond to authority questions and track status.
  5. Approval: collect confirmations/cards and store secure copies.
  6. After approval: set renewal reminders and keep records organised.

Category Breakdown (Simple)

Category Who it is for What matters Notes
A Self-employed in agriculture/animal husbandry Land ownership/lease; adequate capital and income You must show the activity is real and viable
B Self-employed in mining Valid mining licence; sufficient capital/experience Special approvals can apply
C Self-employed in trade/profession Capital investment in a Cyprus business Business plan and financials are often expected
D Self-employed in profession/science Qualifications, premises, sustainable income Local licensing can apply
E Employment in Cyprus Secured role, employer commitment, stable income Employer must be established locally
F Self-sufficient (no work in Cyprus) Stable income/funds abroad; accommodation Common for retirees and self-funded residents

This is a guide only. Final requirements are confirmed case-by-case and by the competent authorities.

FAQs

Informational only — this is not legal or immigration advice. Final decisions rest with competent authorities.

How do Categories A–F differ from Reg. 6(2)?

Reg. 6(2) is an accelerated permanent residence route based on qualifying investment. Categories A–F are traditional routes tied to economic activity, employment, or self-sufficiency.

Which category is most common?

Category F is common for self-sufficient applicants with income from abroad. We confirm fit during scoping.

Can family members be included?

Often yes, depending on the route rules. We confirm eligibility and prepare linked family packs.

Do documents need translation or legalisation?

Often yes. We confirm what is needed based on the issuing country and the authority requirements.

How long does the process take?

Preparation depends on the category and document readiness. Decision timing depends on the authorities.

Nothing here is legal advice; requirements are confirmed case-by-case and by the competent authorities.
