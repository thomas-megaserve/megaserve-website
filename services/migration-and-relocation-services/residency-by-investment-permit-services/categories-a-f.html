Who It’s For

  • Third-country nationals seeking permanent residence through business activity, investment, or self-sufficiency
  • Applicants who need clear guidance on which Category (A–F) fits their intended activity in Cyprus
  • Families who want linked applications and an organised record for renewals and upgrades

We also support Category E (hired employment) where the employment-based route is the correct fit.

What You Get (Deliverables)

  • Route memo confirming the best-fit Category (A–F) and evidence strategy
  • Structured checklist with templates and translation/legalisation guidance
  • Completed filing pack, submission receipts, and a correspondence log
  • Appointment booking coordination (subject to authority calendars)
  • Renewal reminders and an organised archive of filings and evidence

Process & Typical Timeline

  1. Initial scoping: determine the most suitable Category (A–F), household members, and timing.
  2. Checklist & KYC: compile investment/employment/resources, civil status, and address documents.
  3. Build: forms, declarations, legalisations/translations, and evidence pack.
  4. Submit & liaise: file, track, and respond to authority queries.
  5. Decision & issuance: collect approvals/cards and provide secure copies.
  6. Renewal & compliance: monitor conditions and keep a clean archive for future use.

FAQs

How do Categories A–F differ from Reg. 6(2)?

Reg. 6(2) is an accelerated permanent residence route based on qualifying investment. Categories A–F are traditional routes tied to economic activity, employment, or self-sufficiency.

Which category is most common?

Category F is frequently used for self-sufficient applicants with income from abroad. We confirm fit during scoping.

Can dependants be included?

Spouses and minor children are generally eligible, subject to income and accommodation criteria. We coordinate linked applications.

What documents usually need legalisation?

Foreign civil status documents (marriage/birth), police clearances, and some financial proofs often require apostille/legalisation and certified translation.

How long does the process take?

Preparation is typically a few weeks depending on category and document readiness. Decision timelines are set by authorities.
