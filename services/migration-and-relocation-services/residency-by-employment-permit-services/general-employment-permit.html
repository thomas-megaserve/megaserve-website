Who It’s For

  • Cyprus employers hiring non-EU staff for standard roles who need predictable checklists and filing control
  • International groups onboarding staff into Cyprus and needing employer-side setup aligned with local expectations
  • Employers who want renewals and change events tracked, with clean document storage

What You Get (Deliverables)

  • Eligibility scoping note confirming route and role/salary alignment
  • Tailored document checklist and templates (contracts/letters where needed)
  • Completed application pack and submission receipts
  • Authority correspondence log and status updates
  • Arrival onboarding confirmations where included (social insurance/tax identifiers)
  • Renewal schedule and organised file repository

Process & Typical Timeline

  1. Discovery: confirm route, role, sponsor readiness, and timing.
  2. Checklist: gather documents; flag items needing legalisation/translation.
  3. Application build: forms, evidence, employer letters/undertakings.
  4. Submission & liaison: file, track, and respond to authority queries.
  5. Decision & collections: approvals, biometrics/permits as required.
  6. Onboarding: coordinate practical employer and employee steps (as agreed).
  7. Renewals: reminders and clean record-keeping.

Risk & Mitigations

  • Authority timelines: decision times are external; we keep a clean file and track queries to closure.
  • Document standards: legalisation/translation errors cause delays; we provide formats and checklists to reduce resubmissions.
  • Change events: role, salary, or address changes may require updates; we track and advise on steps.
  • Coordination: where legal representation is required, we coordinate with immigration lawyers and can arrange representation/accompaniment through MegaServe.

FAQs

How long does a General Employment application take?

Preparation is typically 2–6 weeks depending on document readiness. Decision times are set by authorities and vary by season and workload.

Can family members be included?

In many cases, dependants can apply on linked routes. We confirm eligibility at scoping and coordinate their documentation and filings.

Do documents need to be legalised and translated?

Often yes. Many foreign-issued documents require legalisation (such as apostille or equivalent) plus certified translation. We provide jurisdiction-specific guidance.

What if the role or salary changes after filing?

Material changes may require an update or re-filing. Notify us immediately so we can advise on the cleanest compliant path.

Do you work with immigration lawyers and attend appointments?

Yes. We can coordinate with immigration lawyers and also provide our own representatives to attend Migration appointments where permitted, so you have a single point of contact.
