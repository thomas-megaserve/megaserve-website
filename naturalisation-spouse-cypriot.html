Naturalisation — Spouse of a Cypriot Citizen

We verify eligibility, assemble marriage and cohabitation evidence, prepare the full application pack, liaise with the authorities, and coordinate the oath through to citizenship issuance.

Overview

This route suits foreign spouses of Cypriot citizens who meet the marriage duration, cohabitation, and good character criteria. MegaServe de-risks your file with an evidence-first approach covering relationship history, joint residence, and civil status consistency.

  • Eligibility memo covering marriage duration and cohabitation thresholds
  • Relationship & residence evidence framework (joint leases, bills, dependants)
  • Application drafting, filing, and authority query handling
  • Oath scheduling, registry updates, and ID/passport follow-through
  • Secure repository with audit trail and renewal/expiry monitoring

Process

  1. Discovery & scoping — confirm marriage duration, cohabitation, and any periods abroad.
  2. Evidence build — relationship history, joint residence proofs, civil status, translations/legalisations.
  3. Application drafting & filing — forms, declarations, fees, and submission.
  4. Authority liaison — respond to queries; schedule interview if required.
  5. Decision & oath — coordinate oath ceremony, registry updates, ID/passport issuance.
  6. Post-approval — guidance on documents and dependent family updates.

FAQ

How long must we be married to apply?
We confirm the current minimum marriage duration and any reduced thresholds where children of the marriage exist.
Do we need to prove cohabitation?
Yes, typically via joint leases/titles, utilities, municipal registrations, and other shared records covering the qualifying period.
Which documents must be legalised or translated?
Foreign-issued civil records (marriage, birth, police clearances) usually require apostille/legalisation and certified translation.
Is an interview required?
Some cases require interview; we prepare you with document cross-checks and typical question frameworks.
How long does the process take?
Preparation is usually 4–12 weeks depending on evidence readiness; authority decision times vary and are outside our control.
